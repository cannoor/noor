Al-Alaq Research Platform

Powerful Features

Built for Islamic scholars, students, and seekers of Qur'anic knowledge who demand more than simple text reading

Advanced Qur'anic Search

Perform complex queries across the Holy Qur'an. Search by Arabic root words, themes, surah chronology, revelation context, and cross-reference with authentic Tafsir literature.

Islamic Knowledge Modules

Add Qur'anic translations, classical Tafsir works, Arabic dictionaries, and Hadith collections as independent packages. Extend with Islamic scholarly tools and plugins.

Comparative Study Interface

View Qur'anic verses alongside multiple translations, classical Tafsir commentaries, and related Hadith. Compare with Torah and Gospel references when contextually relevant.

Islamic Scholarly Annotations

Create detailed notes with Islamic terminology, custom tags for themes like Tawhid, Ibadah, Akhlaq, and link related Ayahs across the Qur'an to build your thematic studies.

Ayah Collection Basket

Collect Qur'anic verses, Hadith quotes, and scholarly notes from various Islamic sources into a centralized workspace for Khutbah preparation, research, or Da'wah materials.

Optimized for Arabic Text

Built with C++ and Qt 6 for superior Arabic text rendering and RTL support. Local SQLite database ensures instant access to Qur'anic content and complete offline functionality.

Three Foundational Pillars

The architectural principles that drive Al-Alaq's design and development

1

Performance & Reliability

Built with C++ and Qt 6 framework for native desktop performance on Windows, macOS, and Linux. Local SQLite database ensures complete offline functionality and instant access.

2

Modularity & Extensibility

Flexible "Lego brick" architecture with independent data packages and tool plugins. Community-driven development for continuous expansion.

3

User-Centric Research

Intuitive workspace designed for serious researchers with advanced annotation tools, powerful search capabilities, and seamless comparative analysis.

Al-Alaq Project

This journey began with my simple question: "How can we leverage modern technology to not just read, but truly *interact* with the Qur'an?" As the initial developer, I envisioned a tool that would feel less like a static book and more like a dynamic, personal research assistant‚Äîa companion for scholars, students, and anyone on a path to deeper knowledge.

Built on a foundation of C++ and the Qt framework, Al-Alaq is engineered for performance, reliability, and complete offline access. I chose to make it open-source (LGPLv3) with the sincere hope that this project will grow beyond a single person's effort. My vision is for a community to form around it, where developers, researchers, and designers from around the world can join in, contributing their skills to create something truly transformative.

This is an invitation to be part of that future. Whether you contribute code, suggest a feature, or simply use the tool in your studies, you are helping to bring this vision to life and transforming it from "my project" into "our project".
